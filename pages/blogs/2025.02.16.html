Winter to Spring: Best Perennials to Plant in February

As February arrives in Vancouver, it's the perfect time to transition your garden from winter dormancy to early spring vibrancy. For perennial enthusiasts, this is the ideal month to get a head start on planting hardy varieties that will reward you with beautiful blooms and lush foliage.


Consider planting hellebores, also known as Christmas Roses, which thrive in cooler temperatures and brighten up any garden with their delicate flowers. 

Snowdrops are another early bloomer, perfect for naturalizing in shady spots. 

If you're looking for color, primroses are a must-have, offering cheerful shades of pink, yellow, and purple.

Lungwort (Pulmonaria) is an excellent option for shady gardens, providing a combination of colorful flowers and speckled foliage. Additionally, heucheras are perfect for adding texture and year-round interest with their striking leaves.

When planting perennials in February, ensure the soil is well-drained and enriched with compost to promote healthy root development. Mulching around the plants will help retain moisture and protect them from fluctuating temperatures.
