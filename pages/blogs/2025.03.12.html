March Gardening in Vancouver: Tips to Tackle Common Concerns


March in Vancouver is a time of transition, as gardeners eagerly prepare for the growing season. However, this month comes with its own set of challenges. Here's how to address common concerns and set your garden up for success:


Unpredictable Weather: Late frosts and heavy rains are still possible. Protect tender plants with row covers and ensure proper drainage to prevent waterlogged soil.


Soil Prep: Winter rains can compact soil and deplete nutrients. Loosen the soil with a fork, add compost, and test pH levels to create a healthy foundation for plants.


Pests and Diseases: Slugs, snails, and aphids become active in damp conditions. Use organic deterrents like diatomaceous earth and encourage beneficial insects. Watch for fungal diseases by improving air circulation and removing debris.


Weed Control: Weeds thrive in early spring. Mulch garden beds and tackle weeds early to prevent them from taking over.


Seed Starting: Start seeds indoors for tomatoes, peppers, and herbs. Ensure they get plenty of light and warmth for strong growth.


Lawn Care: Aerate your lawn to relieve compaction and overseed to fill in bare patches.


By addressing these concerns, you'll be ready to enjoy a thriving garden this spring!
