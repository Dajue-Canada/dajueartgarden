Top Fall Colors: Japanese Maple, Barberry, and Croton in Vancouver

Fall transforms gardens into a vibrant canvas, especially in Vancouver, where certain plants stand out. The Japanese Maple showcases breathtaking hues of crimson and gold, symbolizing change and the beauty of impermanence. Its delicate leaves flutter in the breeze, embodying nature's graceful transition.




The Japanese Barberry adds a striking contrast with its deep burgundy leaves and bright red berries, representing resilience. This plant not only enhances the visual palette but also attracts birds, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.


Garden Croton, with its fiery mix of yellow, orange, and red leaves, offers a tropical flair. Its vivid colors symbolize passion and warmth, brightening up any autumn landscape.


Another popular choice is the Burning Bush, known for its brilliant red foliage that creates a stunning backdrop. This plant symbolizes fortitude and the enduring spirit of nature.


As these plants reach their ultimate splendor, they remind us of the fleeting nature of beauty. Embracing these vibrant colors in your garden can evoke feelings of nostalgia and appreciation for the cycle of life, making fall a truly enchanting season.
